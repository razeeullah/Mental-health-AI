Student Mental Health Analysis Report
As a data scientist, I have analyzed the Student Mental health.csv dataset. Below is the academic breakdown of the variables and their utility in predictive modeling.

üìä Academic Explanation of Columns
Column Header	Academic Description	Data Type
Timestamp	Temporal marker indicating survey submission time.	Temporal (Discrete)
Gender	Standard demographic binary classification.	Categorical (Nominal)
Age	Chronological age of the respondent.	Numerical (Discrete)
Course	Specified field of academic study.	Categorical (Nominal)
Year of Study	Level of progression within the academic program.	Categorical (Ordinal)
CGPA	Standardized academic performance metric (binned).	Categorical (Ordinal)
Marital Status	Indicator of legal relationship status (Social support factor).	Categorical (Binary)
Depression	Self-reported presence of depressive symptoms.	Categorical (Binary)
Anxiety	Self-reported presence of anxiety symptoms.	Categorical (Binary)
Panic Attack	Indicator of acute emotional/physiological distress.	Categorical (Binary)
Specialist Treatment	History of seeking professional clinical intervention.	Categorical (Binary)
üîç Feature & Target Identification
1. Predicting Mental Health Condition
Features (Predictors): Age, Gender, What is your course?, What is your CGPA? (Academic stress), and Marital status (Social support).
Target Variables: Do you have Depression?, Do you have Anxiety?.
2. Predicting Suicide Risk
Direct Variable: N/A (The dataset does not explicitly label suicide risk).
Proxy Predictors: From a clinical perspective, positive indicators in Depression, Anxiety, and Panic attack are the strongest features used to estimate underlying suicide risk in this context.
3. Predicting Emotional State
Primary Feature: Panic attack serves as the indicator for acute emotional states.
Supportive Predictors: CGPA (stress-induced triggers) and Did you seek any specialist? (previous emotional management history).

For machine learning purposes, the primary target variables (labels) are:

Do you have Depression?
